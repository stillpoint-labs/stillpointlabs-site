# Stillpoint Labs Operating Principles: One-Page Summary

*Companion to `2026-05-25-stillpoint-operating-principles.md`. For the wall, the dashboard, the README header.*

## The Thesis

Lift the human. Reduce the load. Refuse the shame frame.

Stillpoint Labs builds software that makes people more capable of the things they already care about, not software that convinces them they should care about new things. Our products coach, partner, and scaffold; they do not surveil, judge, or measure-against.

## The Ten Principles

1. **Stillpoint's thesis.** Lift the human, reduce the load, refuse the shame frame. Every feature gets audited against whether it leaves the household more able to act, or less.

2. **Brand voice.** Clear English. ELI10. Define every term on first use. No talking down. Story-first, metaphor-driven, specific. Banned: *navigate, landscape, leverage, delve, genuinely, honestly, straightforward, to be honest*. The voice is the one asset competitors cannot copy.

3. **Product voice.** Coach, partner, scaffold. Never judge, measure-against, surveil, or shame. *Adjustment*, not *overspending*. *Tend*, not *track*. *Developed*, not *graded*. Silence is reassurance.

4. **The target user is Elisabeth.** A satisficer who values stability over novelty, gets angry at UI changes, uses tech because she has to. We stick the front-end landing on the first try, warn before changes, make the magic invisible on the backend, and default to the smart guess.

5. **The archetype framework.** Archetypes are dimensional, state-dependent, fluid, and inferred from behavior. They are not personality types. The same person occupies different positions on the dimensions as their state shifts. Archetypes are render-time labels, never stored on the user record.

6. **The personalization principle.** Surface preferences from behavioral signals, not from onboarding questionnaires. People are bad at knowing what they want (Gladwell, *Blink*). Ask through tasks not surveys; infer from telemetry; show, then let the user correct.

7. **The mental-load principle.** Working memory holds about four items. Every product surface either reduces or adds to the load. We optimize for reduction. Default to the smart guess. The plan is a guide, not a contract. No forced compliance loops.

8. **The anti-surveillance / anti-shame principle.** No calorie counters. No spend-shaming. No streaks. No comparison shaming. No character-failure framing. No surveillance disguised as personalization. The user can turn off any inferred personalization without breaking the product.

9. **The cross-project learning discipline.** What we learn in one product flows back into the others. Quarterly principles audit per shipped product. Versioned principles doc. New research asks explicitly: does this change anything for the other products.

10. **The moat thesis.** Tech is commoditizing. Boundary-respecting, dignity-preserving UX backed by deep research is the moat. Reveal: *judgment is the moat*. Borg: *cognitive load is the moat*. Troth: *systems-not-character is the moat*. The principles only work if we execute them; execution is the durable distinction.

## The Tensions Worth Naming

**Personalization vs. no surveillance.** We need data to personalize; we said no surveilling. The line: data flow stays user-facing, and the user can turn off any inferred personalization without breaking the product.

**Mental-load reduction vs. honesty about state.** Sometimes the most load-reducing thing the product can do is to *not* show the user the bad news. Reduce the visible task count under stress instead of escalating; never pretend a household is on track when it is not.

**Brand voice rigor vs. accessibility.** ELI10 means use plain English; it does not mean dumb down the idea. Define the technical term inline; trust the reader's intelligence with the concept.

## The Banned Words List

For copy across all surfaces: *discipline, willpower, character, motivation, just need to, should, commitment, grit, smart, optimize, powered by, premium, hack, financial wellness, level up, master your money, navigate, landscape, leverage, delve, genuinely, honestly, straightforward, to be honest*.

## The Daily Question

Before any feature ships, copy gets approved, or notification gets sent: does this leave the household more able to act, or less?

If less, it does not ship.
