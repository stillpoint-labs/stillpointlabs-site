## Key Findings

1. **Yummly's 2024 shutdown is the meal-planning trust cautionary tale.** Whirlpool acquired Yummly in 2017 for $100M; laid off the team April 2024; killed the app December 2024. Same Mint pattern: acquisition + business-model drift → trust collapse → sunset.
2. **Mealime's wedge is minimalism + a usable free tier.** New-user trust is earned by NOT asking too much: a few preference filters, no nutrition surface, fast meal recommendations. The product's restraint IS the trust signal.
3. **Plan to Eat's wedge is family / multi-cook coordination plus a survivor's confidence.** 15 years in market; visible reputation for shared planning. Survival itself is a trust signal in this category.
4. **The meal-planning category's trust stakes are MUCH LOWER than finance.** A bad meal suggestion costs the user nothing but 10 minutes; a bad financial categorization can cost money or trigger shame. This asymmetry shapes the cold-start trust math — Ingle can move faster than Troth can.
