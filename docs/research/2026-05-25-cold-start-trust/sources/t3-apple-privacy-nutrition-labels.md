## Key Findings

1. **Privacy nutrition labels DEMOCRATIZE the trust signal across the App Store.** Apps that respect privacy get a visible "Data Not Collected" indicator, leveling the playing field for small privacy-first apps vs. big surveillance-funded apps.
2. **Adoption was driven by app updates, not voluntary commitment.** 2.7% voluntary adoption vs. 35.3% via mandatory update process. Apple's enforcement matters more than developer commitment.
3. **1 in 3 apps with "Data Not Collected" labels were collecting data (Washington Post audit).** Privacy theater is rampant; self-reported labels without audits don't earn lasting trust.
4. **The label format itself is the design win.** Plain language, structured comparison, prominent placement at install time. This is the canonical "make privacy posture LEGIBLE at the trust-decision moment" pattern.
