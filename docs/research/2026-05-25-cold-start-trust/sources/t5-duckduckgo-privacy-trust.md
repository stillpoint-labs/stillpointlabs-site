## Key Findings

1. **"We don't track you" alone is enough to get the user to install, but not enough to keep them through a controversy.** DuckDuckGo grew to 100M searches/day on the privacy narrative; the 2022 Microsoft-tracker disclosure caused a measurable trust hit because the narrative was undermined.
2. **Trust formation in privacy-first products is asymmetric.** A SINGLE inconsistency between claim and behavior costs more than dozens of consistent claims earn. This is the Sirdeshmukh problem-solving-orientation finding in privacy clothes: the recovery / failure moment dominates trust.
3. **A short, readable privacy policy is itself a competence signal.** "We can explain our privacy posture in 200 words" demonstrates the company has actually thought about it; "we have a 30-page privacy policy" signals legal-defense, not user-care.
4. **Privacy-first products earn trust through ARCHITECTURE, not policy.** Signal's metadata minimization, Proton's open source, DuckDuckGo's no-tracker architecture. Architecture is the structural assurance from McKnight's model.
