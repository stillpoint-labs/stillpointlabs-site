## Key Findings

1. **Trustworthiness has three dimensions, not one.** Operational competence ("they can do the job"), operational benevolence ("they have my interests in mind"), and problem-solving orientation ("when something goes wrong, they fix it"). Competence alone does not produce trust.
2. **Trust mediates the loyalty relationship through perceived value.** The path is: trust → value → loyalty. Trust does not directly produce loyalty; it raises the user's perception of value, which produces loyalty.
3. **Frontline employees and management policies are distinct trust sources.** What the user-facing surface does and what the company's posted policy says are scored independently. Inconsistency between the two collapses trust.
4. **Problem-solving orientation is the asymmetric dimension.** Users notice and weight recovery from failure 2-3x more heavily than they weight nominal competence on a good day. The recovery moment is the trust-defining moment.
