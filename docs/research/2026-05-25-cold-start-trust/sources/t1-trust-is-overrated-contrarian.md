## Key Findings

1. **What businesses call "trust" is often reliability.** Users don't experience an abstract trust calculation; they experience the product doing what it said it would do, repeatedly, with low latency.
2. **"Utility brands win by being testable."** The fastest way to "become" a utility brand is to remove friction and accelerate time-to-value. Brand becomes a shortcut for expected reliability, not an emotional bond.
3. **Deloitte's data: humanity and transparency are overstated vs. capability and reliability.** What people SAY they want from brands (humanity, transparency) doesn't predict what they actually choose; capability and reliability do.
4. **The implication for Stillpoint:** the "no surveys, no surveillance" stance must be paired with VISIBLE COMPETENCE in the first session. Trust signals without competence demonstrations are theater (cf. T5 privacy-theater contrarian). The principle stack is: deliver value + be reliable + (only then) trust-signaling helps.
