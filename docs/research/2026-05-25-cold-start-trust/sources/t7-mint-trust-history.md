## Key Findings

1. **Mint earned initial trust by being free + visually clean + integrating Yodlee bank aggregation.** Pre-Plaid, account aggregation was a major friction point; Mint solved it and grew to 10M+ users.
2. **The 2010 password-storage incident was a structural-trust event.** Mint stored credentials in decryptable format; this was widely reported and damaged the brand. The structural-trust recovery took years (2017 JPM-Chase settlement on tokenized access).
3. **Mint's eventual sunset (2024) is the cautionary tale for trust-after-acquisition.** Intuit's incentive to migrate Mint users to Credit Karma + cross-sell TurboTax violated the unspoken trust contract. The slow decline ended in shutdown.
4. **Mint's pattern: simplicity + visibility + free won initial trust; misaligned business model lost durable trust.** This is the Proton-aligned-business-model finding restated in failure mode.
