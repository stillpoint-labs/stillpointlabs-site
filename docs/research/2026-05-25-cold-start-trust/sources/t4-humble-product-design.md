## Key Findings

1. **"I don't know" becomes "I don't know yet."** The temporal frame turns admission of limitation into a commitment to learn. This is the linguistic move that answers HOW4 most directly.
2. **Humility is a trust signal, not a competence deficit.** When a product says "we don't know your situation yet, but here's a starting guess," users hear honesty + competence + a path forward. When a product pretends to know more than it does, users smell it.
3. **The two-way exchange model.** Onboarding becomes a conversation: the product offers a starting guess, the user corrects it. The exchange itself produces belonging.
4. **The opposite move — overconfident "smart" personalization that turns out to be wrong — destroys trust faster than no personalization at all.** Wrong defaults presented confidently are worse than no defaults.
