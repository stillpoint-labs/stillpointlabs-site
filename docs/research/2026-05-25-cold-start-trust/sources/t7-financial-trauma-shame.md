## Key Findings

1. **Financial trauma is a chronic emotional and physiological response to perceived or real economic insecurity.** It produces freeze responses around money decisions, shame, and disproportionate avoidance.
2. **Banks, credit unions, and financial professionals may seem UNSAFE by default to trauma survivors.** Prior experiences of manipulation, fees-as-punishment, or aggressive collections behavior produce a generalized distrust of the next financial tool.
3. **Trauma patterns can be unlearned.** With consistent, safe interactions, trust can be rebuilt. This is the design opportunity — a financial tool that consistently signals safety can heal as it helps.
4. **The Troth recovery-copy work is the direct application.** "We noticed," "the books," "adjustment" — vocabulary that doesn't trigger the freeze. Versus "you missed," "over budget," "fix this now" — vocabulary that does.
