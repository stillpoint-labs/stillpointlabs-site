## Key Findings

1. **Stated brand-trust preferences (humanity, transparency, values-alignment) do NOT predict behavior over time.** Capability and reliability do.
2. **What people SAY they want from brands ≠ what predicts retention.** Marketing departments over-invest in the stated preferences; retention is driven by the revealed preferences.
3. **For Stillpoint:** the "anti-shame, anti-surveillance, ELI10 voice" positioning is differentiating (because few competitors can ship it) but the actual retention engine is whether the product does the household-finance / meal-planning job correctly and quickly. Trust signaling MUST be paired with competence demonstration.
4. **The interaction effect is asymmetric.** A trust-signaling product without competence loses fast; a competent product without trust signaling can survive but can't acquire the satisficer-Elisabeth user who needs both.
