## Key Findings

1. **Initial trust forms through three INDEPENDENT mechanisms, not one.** (a) **Disposition to trust** — the user's baseline propensity (some users start at "trust until proven otherwise," others at "distrust until proven otherwise"). (b) **Institution-based trust** — situational normality (the product looks like trustworthy products in this category) plus structural assurance (visible safeguards, guarantees, regulation). (c) **Social categorization** — the user infers similarity to themselves or to a trusted group. All three feed into the trust judgment in parallel.
2. **Initial trust strongly predicts willingness to transact.** The path coefficient from initial trust → intention is consistently the highest in the model. A user who does not form initial trust does not buy, does not subscribe, does not provide sensitive data.
3. **Perceived web vendor reputation is the strongest single situational predictor.** "Other people trust them" works as a category-level trust transfer.
4. **Initial trust is fragile.** Once formed, it requires sustained competence demonstrations to stabilize; the first negative event in the first 30 days disproportionately collapses it.

## Inclusion Decision

**Decision:** Core
**Rationale:** Rule 1 (Strong Include) — composite 8.10 ≥ 7.0. McKnight's three-mechanism model is the load-bearing answer to HOW1, HOW2, and HOW5. The disposition-trust finding explains why some users will trust Stillpoint instantly and others will not; the institution-based-trust finding explains why visible structural signals (privacy posture, no-third-party-trackers claim) matter for the latter group; the categorization-trust finding maps directly onto Stillpoint's brand voice + Elisabeth design.
