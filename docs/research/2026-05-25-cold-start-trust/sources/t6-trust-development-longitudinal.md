## Key Findings

1. **Trust evolves through 5 stages: formation, facilitation, establishment, acceleration, reinforcement.** Each stage has different dominant cues. First-session trust (formation) leans on disposition + categorization; later-stage trust (acceleration / reinforcement) leans on consistent competence + demonstrated benevolence over time.
2. **Benevolence and competence remain significant across ALL stages.** They are the persistent dimensions. The Sirdeshmukh / McKnight pillars are durable.
3. **Rule-based trust declines over time.** Users initially trust because the institution looks like other trusted institutions; as the relationship matures, they shift to "this specific actor has demonstrated trustworthiness." Institutional-trust transfers; durable trust is built individually.
4. **Disclosure behavior is a KEY MARKER of the formation → establishment transition.** Users who disclose sensitive information (financial details, health details) signal trust has crossed an internal threshold. The Stillpoint analogue: the user voluntarily connects a bank account, or voluntarily lets the system act on their behalf, when trust has reached the establishment stage.
