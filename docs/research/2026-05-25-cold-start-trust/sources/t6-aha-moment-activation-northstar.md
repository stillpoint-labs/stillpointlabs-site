## Key Findings

1. **Aha moment ≠ activation.** Aha is when the user RECOGNIZES value; activation is when they EXPERIENCE it. The two can happen in the same session or weeks apart.
2. **Activation is the trust threshold predictor.** A user who activates within first 3 days has dramatically higher D30 retention than one who hasn't. The activation point IS the first-trust-formed-here threshold.
3. **Each product has ONE activation metric.** Facebook's was "7 friends in 10 days"; Slack's was "2000 messages sent." The Stillpoint analog should be definable per product.
4. **Activation is testable and trainable.** Onboarding flow improvements that move activation rate by 5-10 points move D30 retention by 30-50%.
