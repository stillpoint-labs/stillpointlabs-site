## Key Findings

1. **Performative privacy collapses trust in BOTH the offending product AND adjacent privacy claims.** When users encounter privacy theater, they develop "justifiable skepticism towards all privacy claims, even legitimate ones."
2. **Privacy claims without architectural backing are theater.** Cookie consent banners that change nothing; privacy nutrition labels that misrepresent collection; differential-privacy claims with no audit. These are recognizable patterns.
3. **The fix is not better claims; it's better architecture.** Signal's Sealed Sender, Proton's open source, Apple's on-device processing — claims backed by architectural impossibility-of-violation.
4. **Trust is asymmetric.** Theater costs more than honest privacy posture earns. The Sirdeshmukh problem-solving-orientation finding restated: failure events dominate trust formation.
