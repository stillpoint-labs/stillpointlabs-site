## Verified Quote(s)

## Key Findings

- Headspace has substantial RCT evidence (multiple trials, 75% positive on depression); Calm at time of review had **zero** RCTs.
- The two market-leading mental-health apps differ dramatically in research-grounding: Headspace publishes outcomes, Calm publishes marketing.
- Conflicts of interest exist in Headspace's trial roster but not at a level that invalidates the findings according to the reviewers.
- Two-tier market: "research-grounded in fact" (Headspace, Woebot, some BCT-based apps) vs "research-grounded in marketing" (Calm, the long tail of meditation apps).
