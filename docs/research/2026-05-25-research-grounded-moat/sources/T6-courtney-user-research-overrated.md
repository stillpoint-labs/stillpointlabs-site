## Key Findings

- The contrarian argument is not "research is bad" but "upfront research is overrated; prototype-tested research wins."
- Design Sprint (4-5 days) is the practical embodiment: skip upfront, prototype, test, iterate.
- The position has high credibility among certain Y-Combinator-shaped practitioners.
- **The nuance for Stillpoint:** the contrarian camp is right that *bad* research is worse than no research, and that *upfront-only* research before any prototype is wasteful. Stillpoint's actual approach is closer to the contrarian camp's pragmatism than to the academic-research-team caricature. The deep-research methodology is fast (1-2 weeks per project), surfaces actionable Recommendations §1, and informs prototypes that get built rather than producing dusty research decks. That is research-grounded *and* lean.
