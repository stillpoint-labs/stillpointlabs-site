## Key Findings

- UX research job postings contracted -73% from 2022 to 2023 per Indeed.
- 11% of UXR respondents personally laid off; 43% lost colleagues; 14% of orgs have no dedicated researcher (2x prior year).
- Industry trend: research roles consolidated into "research-savvy designers" rather than maintained as a specialist function.
- **The counter-intuitive finding for this research:** the field is CONTRACTING, not commoditizing. The competitive landscape is shifting from "every product has a research team" to "most products no longer have one." This *widens* Stillpoint's moat, not narrows it. If competitors are laying off researchers, then "build UX from research first" becomes more differentiated, not less, over the next 2-3 years.
