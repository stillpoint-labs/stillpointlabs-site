## Verified Quote(s)

> "Lean Startup activities talking to customers, collecting preorders and pivoting based on customer feedback correlate positively with a start-up's performance." — Cassens 2021 (synthesis of lean-startup empirical literature). Access: live (PDF).

## Key Findings

- Lean Startup's empirical defense is real but circumscribed: customer-talking + iteration + pivot all correlate positively with performance.
- The review does NOT find that lean replaces research; it finds that lean *integrates* customer feedback into early iteration.
- The "die in the library" failure mode (over-researching, never shipping) is acknowledged across the literature.
- **The reconciliation for this research:** lean and research-grounded are not opposites. Stillpoint's deep-research methodology is fast (~1-2 weeks per project), produces actionable §1 Recommendations, and feeds prototypes that ship. That is lean-shaped. The contrast is with the *anti-research, intuition-only* extreme (Fried/DHH), not with lean iteration.
