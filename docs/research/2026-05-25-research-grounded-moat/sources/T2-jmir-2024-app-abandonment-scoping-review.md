## Key Findings

- Mental-health app retention is catastrophic: 80%+ abandonment in 30 days, 3-4% sustained retention.
- Abandonment drivers cluster into four: poor personalization, generic responses, mismatched expectations, episodic-use design mismatch.
- **The critical finding for Stillpoint:** apps that are clinically effective fail commercially when they reduce need too quickly. Stillpoint's anti-engagement-bait stance is consistent with this finding *only if* the business model survives episodic use. (Subscription survives; engagement-billed does not.)
- The retention crisis is the empirical basis for Stillpoint's principles §3 (Product Voice) and §8 (Anti-Surveillance / Anti-Shame).
