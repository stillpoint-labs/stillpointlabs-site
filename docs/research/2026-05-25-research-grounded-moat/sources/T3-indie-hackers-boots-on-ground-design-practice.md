## Key Findings

- The dominant indie-hacker pattern is closer to Shape Up + Lean Startup + occasional customer-interview rounds. NOT deep-research.
- The deep-research pattern (10-dim source rubric, peer-reviewed lit integration, multi-disciplinary blend) is essentially absent from the indie/boots community.
- This is the key empirical signal for RQ3: the barrier to research-grounding for typical SaaS teams is *not awareness* — they know Cagan, Hall, NN/g — it is *throughput*. Deep research takes time that indie teams don't allocate, even though they have access to all the same methodology references.
- **The moat is the willingness to invest in research throughput when the velocity-game says ship.** That investment is structural (subscription business model, no token-bill, no DAU-on-valuation) for Stillpoint, structurally hostile for everyone else in the comparison set.
