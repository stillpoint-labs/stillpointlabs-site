## Verified Quote(s)

## Key Findings

- 44 real-life case studies of UX-metric-driven design changes; 30 new in 5th edition; 14-year longitudinal collection.
- The institutional UX establishment treats "research-grounded design outperforms intuition" as a settled question — the report exists to quantify, not to argue.
- Companion NN/g article ("Average UX Improvements Are Shrinking Over Time") notes that the per-improvement delta is smaller now than in the 2000s — implies the field has commoditized the easy wins; the remaining wins require deeper research methods.
- The report is a procurement-decision tool — UX leaders use it to defend research budgets internally.
