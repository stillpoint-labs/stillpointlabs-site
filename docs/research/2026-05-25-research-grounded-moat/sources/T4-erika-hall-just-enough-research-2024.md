## Key Findings

- Hall's central claim: research rigor comes from discipline and checklists, not from headcount or academic credentials.
- Just Enough Research has outlasted multiple technology hype cycles (12 years across editions).
- The book frames research as a practice anyone with discipline can do — democratizes the function.
- **Implication for moat-deepening:** the productizable form of Stillpoint's research methodology is closer to Hall's discipline-and-checklists model than to an academic-research-team model. The deep-research skill (10-dimension rubric, source-card template, inclusion-decision matrix) is a discipline-and-checklists artifact. That maps directly to Hall's frame.
- The moat candidate: not the research methodology *per se* (Hall already democratized this), but the *integration of multi-disciplinary frameworks* (BCT taxonomy + polyvagal/state-layer + Daminger cognitive labor + couples therapy outcomes) into a specific consumer-SaaS practice. That integration is the harder-to-copy layer.
