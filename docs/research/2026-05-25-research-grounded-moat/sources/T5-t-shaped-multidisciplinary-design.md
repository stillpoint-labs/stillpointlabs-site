## Key Findings

- The T-shaped concept argues for one deep specialty + broad cross-disciplinary knowledge.
- For research-grounded UX in consumer SaaS: the "T" stem is product/design; the horizontal includes psychology, behavioral econ, family studies, behavior-change science.
- The empirical evidence for T-shape outperformance is mostly anecdotal and case-based; not RCT.
- **For Stillpoint:** the tri-disciplinary blend (physiology + psychology + marital/family) is consistent with the T-shape thesis but the evidence base is thin. The honest claim: Stillpoint's blend is a *hypothesis* about integrated discipline being more durable than single-discipline depth. It is operationally plausible (the principles doc shows it produces specific cross-product wins) but not formally tested.
- The corollary moat-deepening move: invest in the *integration craft* (how disciplines combine in product decisions) rather than depth in any single discipline. The integration craft is rarer than any single-discipline depth.
