## Key Findings

- The cognitive dimension of household labor decomposes into four stages: anticipation, identification, decision-making, monitoring.
- Anticipation (noticing it needs to be done before deciding) is the most invisible and most asymmetric — concentrated on women in 26 of 32 hetero couples.
- This is the foundational research for the marital/family-dynamics layer of Stillpoint's blend (cited explicitly in Ingle May 23 caretaker research and personalization-mechanics research).
- The finding has held up across replications, expansion to same-gender couples, and downstream papers (PMC 2024 on maternal mental health).
- **Implication for moat:** the family-dynamics research base is academically sound, well-corroborated, and operationalizable into specific product decisions (Ingle's "system is the only assignor" principle). This is one of the strongest research-grounded discipline layers Stillpoint operates in.
